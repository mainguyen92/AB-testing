Introduction¶

The dataset contains information about patients who have made medical appointments, as well as whether or not they have showed up at the scheduled time. Included in the data are the patients' health conditions, such as having or not diabetes, hypertensions and alcoholism. Other generic characteristics like genders, addresses and ages are also available. In this analysis, we will provide some exploratory visualizations for the correlation between such variables and the probability that the patient did not show up at the appointments. After that, research questions as follows will be explored:

  1. Is there a relationship between Age and No-show probability?
  2. Does the average age differ between male and female patients?
  3. Is there a relationship between hypertension and the show-up/no-show status?

# Exploring the percentage of no-show patients with respect to 2 metric variables: Age and Waiting time

def NoShowProbMetric(group_by):
    dmt = pd.crosstab(index = df[group_by], columns = df['Status']).reset_index()
    dmt['NoshowProb'] = dmt['No'] / (dmt['Yes'] + dmt['No'])
    dmt.columns.name = None
    return dmt[[group_by, 'NoshowProb']]


sns.lmplot(data = NoShowProbMetric('Age'), x = 'Age', y = "NoshowProb")

# Exploring the percentage of no-show patients with respect to categorical variables relating to medical history: Hypertension, 
# Diabetes, Alcoholism

def NoshowProbCat(group_by):
    rows = []
    for item in group_by:
        for level in df[item].unique():
            row = {'Condition':item}
            Noshow = len(df[(df[item] == level) & (df['Status'] == 'No')])/len(df[df[item] == level])
            row.update({'Level': level, 'Noshowprob': Noshow})
            rows.append(row) 
    return pd.DataFrame(rows)

Research Question 1: Is there a relationship between Age and No-show probability?¶

From the plot, we can hypothesize that Age is negatively associated with No-show probability.

y = NoShowProbMetric('Age')['NoshowProb'] # y is the probability of no-show, the dependent variable
X = sm.add_constant(X)

model = sm.OLS(y, X).fit()
predictions = model.predict(X)

Conclusions¶

In this analysis, 3 research questions are addressed. By performing t-test, OLS regression and Chi-square test of independence, we yield three findings:

  • The higher age of the patient is associated with lower probability of no-show. Possible explations for this is that older patients tend to pay more attention to health checks than younger people since high age is normally correlated with the risk of illness. In addition, older people is more likely to suffer from symptoms, aches and pains that need more urgent medical attention, forcing them to show up at appoitments that they have scheduled.
  • The average age of female patients is higher than male patients. This can imply that the health of women is better than that of men, since women appear to face medical problems at a higher age than men. It can be explained by the healthier lifestile and more serious disease prevention of women compared to men.
  • There is an association between whether the patients have hypertension and whether they show up at the appointment. The fact that hypertension requires regular check-ups can explain this finding.
